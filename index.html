The Four Rules of Layout

Emptiness

A simple rule that's often broken: place elements with similar meaning close together and surround them with empty space. Emptiness is a powerful design tool.

Grid

The sizes of elements and the spaces between them fit into a harmonious layout — it's called a grid. If an element is out of place, the viewer may notice it.

Fonts

No more than three fonts on a website. One for headings, another for all other text, and a third for highlighting important words. That should be enough.

Colors

Usually, a website has two colors: a primary color and an accent color. A good color pair can be chosen through experience or with the help of special tools.
